Bioimaging

Simulation and modeling of nano-structure to absorb or reflect light inspired by butterflies

Introduction

In this project, I learned to use finite-difference time-domain (FDTD) software from Dr. Wang. I cooperated with Dr. Wang to build different models that inspired by the butterfly wings. After the calculation of the models, I did the data analysis, helping write the paper. This work showed the different structure on butterfly wings that enhances the absorption of different wavelength light.

 

Related publication

1. Wang W, Zhang W, Fang X, Huang Y, Liu Q, Bai M, et al. Omnidirectional light absorption of disordered nano-hole structure inspired from Papilio ulysses. Optics Letters. 2014;39:4208-11 (LINKS)

2. Wang W, Zhang W, Fang X, Huang Y, Liu Q, Gu J, et al. Demonstration of higher colour response with ambient refractive index in Papilio blumei as compared to Morpho rhetenor. Scientific reports. 2014;4. (LINKS)

 

Instructor: Zhang Wang

Investigator: Wang Wanlin, Fang Xiaotian, Huang Yiqiao

 

Project Description

Butterflies routinely produce nanostructured surfaces with useful properties. We investigate two different structures. One is disordered nano-hole structure with ridges inspired from Papilio ulysses produce omnidirectional light absorption comparing with the common ordered structure. The other is multilayer structures are known to produce vivid iridescent colouration in many butterflies.
